<Section icon={Target} title="Mastering Combat and Skill Acquisition">

Combat in **Dragon Ball Xenoverse 2 Nintendo Switch** is an evolution of the arena brawler genre. While it may seem like a button-masher initially, the mid-game introduces significant difficulty spikes that require precise timing, stamina management, and a deep move pool. You don't just "unlock" moves by leveling up; you must actively seek out legendary characters to teach you.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Locate an Instructor">
    Find iconic characters like Krillin, Vegeta, or Piccolo stationed around Conton City. They appear as icons on your map after certain story milestones.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Pass the Entrance Exam">
    Each mentor requires you to pass a basic combat test to prove your worth. This usually involves performing specific combos or using their signature move.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Complete Training Lessons">
    Mentors offer four distinct lessons. Completing these will grant you their Super Attacks and, eventually, their Ultimate Attack (e.g., Final Flash or Destructo Disc).
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Master the Skill">
    Use the newly acquired skills in Parallel Quests to increase your proficiency and unlock further advanced training opportunities.

The lock-on system can be finicky on the Switch. It doesn't always target the nearest enemy. Practice using the right stick to cycle targets quickly, especially during multi-enemy Parallel Quests where positioning is everything.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Multiplayer and Parallel Quests">

The heart of the longevity in **Dragon Ball Xenoverse 2 Nintendo Switch** lies in its online community. The game feels like a "Dragon Ball Universe," where players congregate to take on challenges together. Parallel Quests (PQs) are the primary way to farm for rare clothing, equipment, and skills.

- **Offline PQs**: Great for practicing with AI teammates and grinding early levels.
- **Online PQs**: Join up to two other players to tackle high-difficulty missions with better drop rates.
- **Expert Missions**: 6-player raids against massive bosses like Great Ape Vegeta.

| Activity | Player Count | Difficulty | Primary Rewards |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Story Mode** | Solo | Scaling | Progression / Unlocks |
| **Parallel Quests** | 1-3 Players | Low to High | Skills / Outfits / Zeni |
| **Expert Missions** | 1-6 Players | Very High | TP Medals / Rare Souls |
| **Online Battles** | 1v1 / 3v3 | Competitive | Ranking / Bragging Rights |

<Callout type="success" title="Server Stability">
In 2026, the Switch servers remain highly active. Matchmaking for Parallel Quests is generally fast. If you encounter "stalling" matches, check your NAT type in the Switch internet settings; NAT Type A or B is required for a stable Xenoverse experience.
</Callout>
