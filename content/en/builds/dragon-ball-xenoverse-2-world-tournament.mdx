<Section icon={Target} title="Quest Points and Ranking Mechanics">

The primary objective during any tournament is to accumulate Quest Points (QPs). Your total QP determines your placement on the global leaderboard, which is displayed and updated in real-time on the green Newsboard in the Time Machine area of Conton City. The matchmaking system paired with the point allocation system creates a high-stakes environment where a single loss can set back hours of progress, especially once you cross specific point thresholds.

Understanding how points scale is crucial for optimizing your climb. The system utilizes a soft cap at 1600 QPs, completely altering the risk-reward ratio of every match you accept.

| QP Threshold | Win Points | Loss Points | Progression Strategy |
| Under 1600 QP | 40 QP minimum | 1 to 3 QP | Aggressive matchmaking, fast fights |
| Over 1600 QP | 10 QP minimum | 5 to 10 QP | High-risk matches, target top ranks |

When you are below 1600 QPs, the system is highly forgiving. Defeating an opponent with a similar or lower point count guarantees at least 40 QPs, while losing only docks a negligible 1 to 3 points. This allows players to climb rapidly through volume play. However, once you cross the 1600 QP milestone, the baseline shifts. Wins against lower-ranked players yield only 10 QPs, while a loss against them can strip away 10 QPs. To progress efficiently past this point, you must target players with higher QP totals to maximize your gains and minimize your losses.
